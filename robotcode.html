What is a Robot?

Source: Tech Policy Lab, University of Washington

Robotics is a vast field, however the definition of what a robot truely is can be narrowed down to the notion that: "A robot is an autonomous machine capable of sensing its environment, carrying out computations to make decisions, and performing actions in the real world." - Erico Guizzo

History of Robotics

Source: WIRED

  • 320 BCE: Aristotle
  • 1495: Leonardo Da Vinci’s Mechanical Knight
  • 1913: Henry Ford’s Assembly Line
  • 1920: Karl Capek’s “Rossums Universal Robots”
  • 1932: Lilliput
  • 1942: Isaac Asimov’s “Runaround”
  • 1950: The Turing Test
  • 1954: Unimate
  • 1956: Unimation
  • 1966: Shakey
  • 1979: The Stanford Cart
  • 1993: Dante
  • 1999: Sony Aibo
  • 2000: Honda ASIMO
  • 2002: Roomba
  • 2012: NASA’s R2
  • 2012: Toyota Prius and Google
  • 2015: Sofia

Applications of Robotics Today



There are many different applications of robotics in today's society, from improving large scale industrial tasks, to entertaining kids through toy robots. Some of the main types of use are industrial robots, domestic robots, medical robots, military robots, space robots and hobby / enteratinment robots.
