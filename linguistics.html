Computing in the Field of Linguistics

The field is concered with the interpretation and processing of natural language for wide application to a multitude of problems.

  • Speech Recognition
  • Speech Synthesis
  • Translation
  • Sentiment/Tone Analysis
  • Study of Language Evoloution

Computational linguistics relies greatly on cross discipline collaboration between computer scientists and fields such as linguistics, mathematicians, psycholinguists and artificial intelligence researchers, to name a few. This collaboration is essential to be able to transform the vastly complex sphere of natural language processing into actionable progress in the field.

Perhaps on of the most prevalent and recognizable forms of computational linguistics is that of machine translation, a widely available technology through sites such as Google Translate. Originally machine translation was rather simple, performing substitutions of words between languages, however given the complexity of syntax rules, and sentence structure between different languages the results are not always reliable. As such interdisciplinary work on the topic quickly began, and the field of computational linguistics was born. Today cutting edge technology includes work in the area of neural networks and deep learning methods, to be able to abstract the syntax evaluation and deliver better translations. Google moved to neural network translation for Google Translate in 2016 (Google Neural Machine Translation System.)

Other work includes human-to-machine translation, this instead of aiming to generate a target text aims simply to understand the natural language, spoken or written by humans and allow instructions to be interpreted by the computer. Popular examples include: Apple's Siri, Google Assistant, or Amazon's Alexa.
